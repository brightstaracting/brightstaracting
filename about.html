Originally from Ukraine, Stalina Guberchenko is a professional actress, theater educator, and the founder of Bright Star Acting Studio in Brooklyn, New York. She is a graduate of New York University Abu Dhabi and NYU Tisch School of the Arts, where she continued her professional training at the Lee Strasberg Theatre & Film Institute focused on Method Acting, Acting for Film & TV, Character Development, Movement, Voice and Speech, and Stage Combat.

Her stage experience includes Fair Winds and Winds of War at Theater for the New City, The Caucasian Chalk Circle at the NYUAD Arts Center, The Duchess of LES at the Kraine Theatre, Your Faithful Reader at Teatro LATEA, and Kholstomer, Omar Khayyam, and Equus at Essence Theater. She also performed in multiple theater installations at the Abu Dhabi Art Festival and Expo Dubai.

On screen, Stalina has played lead roles in short films such as Just a Little Longer, Mom Said Always Be Nice, and Snarly Yow. She appeared in Ukrainian TV series Vskritie Pokazhet and Slipa, and continues to act in commercials, brand projects, and music videos.

Beyond acting, Stalina has extensive experience teaching and inspiring children worldwide. She has worked as an acting instructor, creative director, and theater educator at childrenâ€™s camps including Suzirya Tauriia (Ukraine), Talentland (New York), and Jugendhaus (Germany), developing creative programs that build imagination and confidence through performance.

Fluent in English, Russian, and Ukrainian, and proficient in German, Stalina brings a global perspective and passion for storytelling to her teaching. Through Bright Star Acting Studio, she helps every child discover their inner spark and shine in their own unique way.
