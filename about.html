Professional actress and theater educator based in Brooklyn, New York.

Originally from Ukraine, Stalina Guberchenko is a professional actress, theater educator, and founder of Bright Star Acting Studio in Brooklyn, New York. She is a graduate of NYU Abu Dhabi and NYU Tisch School of the Arts, completing her training at the Lee Strasberg Theatre & Film Institute.

Her stage work includes productions at Theater for the New City, Kraine Theatre, and Teatro LATEA, as well as international performances at Expo Dubai and the Abu Dhabi Art Festival.

On screen, Stalina has appeared in short films such as Just a Little Longer, Mom Said Always Be Nice, and Snarly Yow, along with Ukrainian TV series Vskritie Pokazhet and Slipa.

She has taught acting and creative arts in Ukraine, New York, and Germany, helping children build imagination, focus, and confidence through play and performance.

Fluent in English, Russian, and Ukrainian, and proficient in German, she brings an international perspective and heartfelt passion for storytelling to every class at Bright Star.
